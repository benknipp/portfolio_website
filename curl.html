Project Brief

Days on the water are meant to be everything but stressful therefore it's good practice to check the conditions of the wind, waves, and weather near the body of water where we will be having fun.

Existing weather apps aren't meeting the goal of displaying complex weather data in an accessible, easy-to-comprehend way, especially when it comes to surfing.

This project seeks to rectify this. By applying best practices for UX we want to explore what an app designated for surfers has to look like to let them enjoy their favourite past-time.

One of the key issues with existing apps is that the display of weather data to the user is very complex. An app with simple display of information and easy to understand content will serve as the main focal point in the research and design effort.

Design Fidelity

Launch Screen

The launch screen and logo design has gone through various iterations. These iterations saw alternating placements, inclusion of the launch screen as part of the on-boarding process and separation from the on-boarding in the final iterations.

Starting with the high-fidelity stages and before approaching usability testing, the logo was updated and the app name was changed to Curl from the working title of VELA to make the app more distin- guishable.

The app name Curl is derived from surfer lingo and refers to the section of the wave face that curls over, right before it breaks. Surfers try to stay as close to the curl as possible since it is the most power- ful part of the wave.

On-Boarding

The on-boarding process and corresponding screens has remained true to it’s core with updates in the high-fidelity stages to allow for easier display of the core features and since the four icon square of the initial designs, which was also used on the home screen for navigation, was abandoned due to duplication of functions and bad UX design.

Forecast Screen

The forecast screen posed considerable challenge in order to find a balance between displaying the necessary details while not overcrowding the individual screens.

While the initial idea of separate screens was soon accomodated by the use of a segmented control, the number of data fields was quite significant. In order to make the screens more easy to digest, the data points only reflect one day (initially up to 7) and the map has been abolished in order to gain screen retail space for the placement of icons and text. A date picker allows for the data selection of individual days.

As a result of usability testing and the review for accessibility the number of graphical elements to display information was increased substantially.

A/B Testing

Based on feedback during the usability test study for Curl, enhancements and updates to the design were proposed for the next iteration.

To ensure that these updates would be preferential in design to the screens in the usability test a preference test was conducted.

The preference test results are clearly indicating that the updated version is the more preferred with 19 of 22 participants choosing the updated screen over the screen used during the usability test.

The difference is deemed 99 % likely to be statistically significant. The updated screen will therefore be incorporated into the next iteration of the Curl prototype.
